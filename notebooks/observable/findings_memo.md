# Missouri Vehicle Stops: The Hispanic Surge
## Findings Memo — March 2026

The five years of mandatory state data show a systematic increase in traffic enforcement against Hispanic drivers— not just in volume, but in stop  intensity. In 2020, Hispanic drivers were stopped at the lowest per-capita rate of any racial group: 11.3 stops per 100 residents, compared with 18.6 for white drivers and 30.2 for Black drivers. By 2024, the Hispanic rate had climbed to 13.5 — a 19 percent increase that outpaced every other group.

## per-capita rate

Hispanic drivers went from the lowest per-capita stop rate of any group (11.3 per 100 residents in 2020) to nearly matching white drivers (13.5 vs. 20.7 in 2024). Others, IE white and Black rates barely moved.

Missouri's Hispanic population grew about 32% between 2020 and 2024 (262,677 → 346,700, ACS). Stops grew 57%. The stops outpaced population growth nearly 2 to 1.

## 2020  being the baseline is not the problem!

Hispanic per-capita stop rate was 11.3 in 2020 and 2021, climb doesn't start until 2022. Whether you use 2020 or 2021 as the baseline, the Hispanic per-capita increase is 5–6× ish  larger than for white or Black drivers.

Hispanic year-over-year stop growth: +6.7% (2021), +13.9% (2022), +18.4% (2023), +9.2% (2024). The increase accelerated every year through 2023 before moderating.

## Discretionary stops

The increase is concentrated in stop categories where officers have the most discretion:

| Stop reason | Hispanic 2020 | Hispanic 2024 | Change | White change |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| License | 5,278 | 12,080 | +129% | — |
| Investigative | 985 | 1,868 | +90% | -15% (fell) |
| Equipment | 3,462 | 6,062 | +75% | — |
| Moving violation | 20,879 | 28,989 | +39% | — |

Investigative stops of Hispanic drivers nearly doubled while investigative stops of white drivers fell (29,565 → 25,158).

## It's not just one agency

MSHP accounts for 24% of all stops. Removing them makes the trend stronger:

- MSHP Hispanic stop change: +44%
- All other agencies: +64%

MSHP in 2024: Hispanic search rate 6.2% v  white 3.2% (1.9×). Hispanic arrest rate 5.1% vs. white 2.7% (1.9×). MSHP has never submitted comments to the AG explaining its disparities.

## Searches and arrests

In 2020, Hispanic drivers were searched at a lower rate than white drivers (7.63% vs. 8.30%). By 2021, the lines crossed. By 2024, Hispanic drivers were searched at the highest rate: 6.40% vs. 4.68% for white.

The Hispanic arrest rate climbed from 4.80% to 6.17%. The white arrest rate fell from 3.70% to 3.38%. The Hispanic-to-white arrest ratio went from 1.3× to 1.8×.

## Stopped more, BUT found less

Hispanic searches grew 32% (2,268 → 2,988) while contraband found fell 28% (798 → 574). Hit rates dropped for all groups, but only Hispanic drivers faced more searches despite the declining yield. White and Black searches both fell (~35%).

## 287(g)'s context

More than 60 Missouri agencies have signed 287(g) agreements with ICE. MSHP signed in March 2025; the program went operational September 2025. Under the task force model, officers can ask about immigration status during routine traffic stops.

The Hispanic stop pattern was already accelerating from 2022–2024, before most agreements were signed.  SO, was the enforcement culture already shifting before the formal agreements made it policy???

Each year the AG invites agencies to explain their data. In 2020–2023, roughly 500 agencies were included in the comment file. In 2024, just 18 responded. MSHP — the state's largest agency — has never submitted a substantive comment. Agencies with the sharpest Hispanic stop increases went weirdly silent.
